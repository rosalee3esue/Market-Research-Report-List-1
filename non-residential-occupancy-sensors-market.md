<p><p>The non-residential occupancy sensors market comprises various types: PIR (Passive Infrared) sensors detect motion by sensing changes in infrared radiation, ideal for areas with consistent heat sources. Ultrasonic sensors use sound waves to detect movement, effective in complex spaces. Dual-technology occupancy sensors combine PIR and ultrasonic technologies, enhancing accuracy by reducing false triggers. Other sensors may include microwave and photoelectric options, catering to diverse applications in commercial settings, optimizing energy efficiency, security, and operational effectiveness in non-residential environments.</p></p>
