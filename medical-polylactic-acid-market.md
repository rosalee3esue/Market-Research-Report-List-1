<p><p>The Medical Polylactic Acid market comprises three primary types: L-Polylactic Acid (PLLA), D-Polylactic Acid (PDLA), and DL-Polylactic Acid (PDLLA). PLLA, characterized by its crystallinity and slower degradation rate, is widely used in sutures and implants. PDLA, with a faster degradation rate, is used in applications requiring quicker tissue regeneration. PDLLA, a blend of both isomers, offers versatility in applications, balancing mechanical properties and biodegradation rates. Together, these types cater to diverse medical applications, enhancing patient care and recovery.</p></p>
